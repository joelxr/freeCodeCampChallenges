Sir Alexander Fleming

Alexander Fleming's timeline

Click here to read more about one of the most scientist of all time.
  • 1881 Was born in Ayrshire, Scotland;
  • 1900 Signed up the Volunteer Force of London Scotish Regiment as a private;
  • 1903 Enrolled at St Mary's Hospital Medical School in Paddington;
  • 1906 Qualified with a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery degree from the school with distinction;
  • 1908 Gained a Bachelor of Science degree with Gold Medal in Bacteriology;
  • 1914 Served throughout World War I as a captain in the Royal Army Medical Corps;
  • 1923 Discovered the enzyme lysozyme;
  • 1928 Discovered the penicillin and was elected Professor of Bacteriology of the University of London;
  • 1929 Published his discovery of penicillin, in the British Journal of Experimental Pathology;
  • 1945 Shared the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with Howard Florey and Ernst Boris Chain
  • 1951 Was elected the Rector of the University of Edinburgh;
